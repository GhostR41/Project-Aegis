Inspiration

Our inspiration stems from the desire to bridge the gap between complex orbital mechanics and accessible visualization. We wanted to create a space where data becomes a navigable, intuitive experience rather than just raw numbers.

About this project

Project Aegis is an advanced interface for orbital and systems analysis. It integrates cutting-edge visualization with real-time data feeds to simulate and interpret environments seamlessly.

How this helps

By providing a transparent, accurate, and easy-to-use platform, we aim to heighten global awareness, readiness, and strategic planning capabilities for complex systems and potential events.

NASA DART Mission

The Double Asteroid Redirection Test (DART) was the first-ever mission dedicated to investigating and demonstrating one method of asteroid deflection by changing an asteroid's motion in space through kinetic impact.

Advanced Targeting

DART's solely carried instrument, DRACO (Didymos Reconnaissance and Asteroid Camera for Optical navigation), captured images of the asteroid Didymos and its moonlet Dimorphos to support the spacecraft's autonomous guidance system.

Too Late to Act

Recent incidents where asteroids were discovered too late — a stark reminder of why planetary defense matters.

February 15, 2013

Chelyabinsk Meteor

An 18-meter asteroid exploded over Chelyabinsk, Russia at 30 km altitude. The shockwave damaged 7,200 buildings and injured 1,500 people. It was completely undetected before entry.

July 25, 2019

Asteroid 2019 OK

A 100-meter "city killer" asteroid flew past Earth at just 65,000 km — one-fifth the distance to the Moon. It was discovered only hours before its closest approach, coming from the Sun's direction.

October 7, 2008

Asteroid 2008 TC3

Discovered just 20 hours before impact, this 4-meter asteroid exploded 37 km above Sudan's Nubian Desert. The first asteroid ever predicted to hit Earth — but barely in time.

February 13, 2023

Asteroid 2023 CX1

Discovered less than 7 hours before impact by a single Hungarian astronomer. It entered over Normandy, France producing a brilliant fireball visible across the English Channel.
